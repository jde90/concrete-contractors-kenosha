What's different about pouring concrete in Twin Lakes

Twin Lakes is a lakeside community in western Kenosha County centered around Lake Elizabeth and Mary Lake. The village is a mix of seasonal cottages and year-round homes, many of which sit close to the water. That proximity to the lakes creates a specific set of challenges for concrete work: elevated water tables that can saturate subgrade soils, shoreline erosion that affects grading near the water, and the constant freeze-thaw cycle that's amplified by moisture proximity.

For lakefront properties in Twin Lakes, we adjust our approach significantly. Base prep often requires additional drainage consideration -- French drains or gravel channels beneath the slab to prevent hydrostatic pressure from lifting the concrete. We spec 4,500 PSI air-entrained concrete (6% entrained air) as standard, with a vapor barrier on any slab that will be enclosed or finished. Boat ramp pads and dock approaches get a broom finish for traction and thickened edges to handle trailer loads. The 48" frost depth and ~100 annual freeze-thaw cycles mean every detail of the pour matters even more when water is close.

Cold-weather pours in Twin Lakes

We pour year-round when the project schedule demands it, using calcium-free accelerating admixtures, insulated blankets, and propane heaters in tents. Cold pours add 8-15% to total cost and are slower, but for a builder under deadline they're routinely the right call. October-March pours we monitor with embedded thermocouples to keep the concrete above 40 degrees F for the first 72 hours per ACI 306.
