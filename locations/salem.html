What's different about pouring concrete in Salem

Salem sits in southwest Kenosha County along the Fox River corridor, part of the Silver Lake-Salem-Trevor triangle that makes up the quieter, more established western side of the county. Many homes here date from the 1970s through the early 2000s, which means driveways, patios, and sidewalks that have endured 20-40+ years of Wisconsin freeze-thaw are now cracking, heaving, and spalling beyond what patching can fix.

The Fox River area presents specific challenges: higher water tables in low-lying sections near the river, mixed soil profiles that range from sandy loam to heavier clay depending on the parcel, and established trees whose root systems can undermine slab edges. For Salem pours we start with a site inspection that includes checking drainage grades, probing soil conditions, and planning around existing root zones. We spec 4,500 PSI air-entrained concrete (6% entrained air) over a minimum 4" compacted Class II base, with deeper excavation where soft or organic soils require it.

Cold-weather pours in Salem

We pour year-round when the project schedule demands it, using calcium-free accelerating admixtures, insulated blankets, and propane heaters in tents. Cold pours add 8-15% to total cost and are slower, but for a builder under deadline they're routinely the right call. October-March pours we monitor with embedded thermocouples to keep the concrete above 40 degrees F for the first 72 hours per ACI 306.
