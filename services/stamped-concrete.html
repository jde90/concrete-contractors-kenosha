What's included in our stamped pour

Stamped concrete is more involved than a standard pour, done right, it lasts 25+ years; done cheaply, it fades and flakes within five. Every stamped installation we do in Kenosha includes:

  • Excavation, base prep, and forms the same as any pour, sloped, compacted, edge-formed.
  • 4,000 PSI air-entrained mix with integral color tinted at the truck before pour.
  • Synthetic fiber reinforcement (or rebar on slabs over 400 sq ft).
  • Color release agent broadcast across the surface for highlight and antiquing.
  • Stamping with overlapping mat patterns, hand-tooled at borders and joints.
  • Detail trowel work on grout lines and pattern transitions.
  • Sawcut control joints placed in the lowest-visibility lines of the pattern.
  • Two coats of UV-stable acrylic sealer with grit additive after 28-day cure.
  • Maintenance plan, written, dated, with resealing schedule.

Pattern options

We carry mats for the four pattern families that complement the Midwest home aesthetic:

  • Random flagstone. Irregular natural-stone look. Most popular for backyard patios.
  • Ashlar slate. Rectangular cut-stone, more formal. Common on front walkways and entries.
  • Roman / European cobble. Smaller stone units, old-world look. Great for accents and borders.
  • Wood-plank. 6-inch or 12-inch plank patterns, modern aesthetic, popular on newer Pleasant Prairie and Somers builds.

We bring pattern boards to every on-site estimate so you can see and touch the texture before committing.

Color & sealing notes for Wisconsin

Three things our climate does to stamped concrete: bleach the color (UV), surface-spall (freeze-thaw + chlorides), and fade the sealer (moisture cycles). We counter all three with:

  • Integral color, not surface stain. Pigment goes into the full depth of the pour so chips don't show as light spots.
  • UV-stable acrylic sealer. Generic sealers yellow and peel in 18 months, we use a UV-rated product with a 3-5 year service life.
  • Grit additive in sealer. Brings traction up to broom-finish level so wet stamped patios are safe.
  • Re-seal every 2-3 years. The single biggest determinant of lifespan. We offer a flat-rate maintenance plan or do it as a one-off.

Stamped concrete FAQs

How much does stamped concrete cost in Kenosha?

Stamped concrete runs $14-$22 per square foot installed in 2025. Patterns with multiple colors, custom borders, or three-dimensional textures land at the upper end. A typical 250 sq ft stamped patio runs $3,500-$5,500.

How long does stamped concrete last in Wisconsin?

Properly installed and maintained stamped concrete lasts 25 to 30 years in Southeast Wisconsin. The biggest factors are mix quality (4,000 PSI air-entrained minimum), proper jointing, and resealing every 2-3 years. Skipping the resealing typically cuts life in half.

Does stamped concrete get slippery when wet?

It can be more slippery than broom finish, especially with smooth stone patterns. We add a grit additive to the sealer on every stamped installation that brings traction back to broom-finish levels with no visual change.

Can stamped concrete be poured over existing concrete?

Yes, using a stamped overlay system 1/2 to 1 inch thick if the existing slab is sound. If the underlying slab is cracked, sinking, or under 3 inches thick, the overlay will inherit those problems and we recommend tearing it out instead.

What patterns are most popular here?

Ashlar slate and random flagstone are the two most popular patterns in Kenosha because they complement the Midwest home aesthetic. Wood-plank patterns are gaining popularity for modern homes.
